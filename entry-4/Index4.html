The Kakapo, a parrot owl native to New Zealand, ranks at the top of animals thought to be the most stupid.

In short, a large species of flightless bird that lives on the ground at night has little value for its own life. Thus, the stupid part.

Imagine the predator sighting the prey. The predator could advance slowly. Or the animal might sprint quickly over the ground. Doesn't matters. The predator attacks in either scenario. You may imagine the animal as a cat, stoat, rat, or anything else. The Kakapo is the prey. The Kakapo is static. There is no blinking. Even if it notices the attack coming from a different continent.

The bird froze and is now waiting to be eaten.
